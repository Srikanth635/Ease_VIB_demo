Research Timeline

Explore the milestones and achievements of EASE

EASE CRC Initiation (Phase I)

Initiation of the EASE CRC, focusing on understanding and replicating human everyday activities in robotics.

CRAM-I Development (Phase I)

Developed the first version of the Cognitive Robot Abstract Machine (CRAM), enabling robots to perform complex manipulation tasks.

KnowRob2.0 Introduction (Phase I)

Introduced KnowRob2.0, an advanced knowledge representation and reasoning framework for robotic agents.

NEEM Hub Establishment (Phase I)

Established the NEEM Hub, a data storage system for Narrative-enabled Episodic Memories, facilitating knowledge extraction from robot experiences.

CRAM-II Architecture (Phase II)

Developed the Cognitive Robot Abstract Machine (CRAM-II), enabling advanced reasoning and action planning in everyday scenarios.

Generative AI Integration (Phase II)

Introduced generative AI techniques, allowing robots to predict, adapt, and improve task execution dynamically.

Virtual Research Building (VRB) (Phase II)

Launched the VRB, a digital twin environment for simulation, training, and collaborative research in robotics.

AICOR Institute Established (Phase II)

Founded the AICOR Institute to ensure sustainability and drive innovation in AI-powered robotics.

Enhancing CRAM's Cognitive Capabilities (Phase III)

To equip CRAM with advanced cognitive abilities to enable seamless human-robot interaction and co-construction.

Data-Driven Everyday Activity Science and Engineering (Phase III)

To leverage data-driven approaches to understand and model human behavior in everyday activities, informing the development of intelligent robot agents.

Co-Construction Capable Robot Agents (Phase III)

To design and develop robot agents that can effectively collaborate with humans in various tasks, adapting to their needs and preferences.

Maria and Her Robot Assistant

Maria, a retired teacher recovering from an ankle injury, uses a robot that assists in daily tasks. The robot adapts to her preferences in real-time and improves based on feedback.


Robot: Good morning, Maria. I've prepared your coffee station. Your mug, sugar, and milk are ready for you.

Maria: Thank you. That's very helpful.

Maria: I'd like to work on my bonsai garden today. Can you assist me with that?

Robot: Certainly, Maria. What kind of style are you aiming for?

Maria: I prefer a more natural look.

Robot: Understood. I'll adjust my techniques accordingly. Please let me know if you'd like to make any adjustments.

Maria: It looks a bit too symmetrical.

Robot: You're right. I'll focus on creating a more asymmetrical and organic shape. Perhaps we could add some interesting twists and turns to the branches?

Maria: That sounds good.

Robot: Shall we start with this bonsai here?

Maria: Yes, please.

Robot: Let's prepare lunch now. I can fetch the ingredients and do the chopping. You can focus on the mixing and seasoning.

Maria: That would be great, thank you.

Robot: Dinner is ready. How was your day?

Maria: It was a good day. Thank you for your help.

Robot: I'm glad I could assist you. I've taken note of your feedback on the bonsai styling. I've also found some interesting techniques online that you might enjoy. Would you like to explore them tomorrow?

Assistive Robots in Elderly Care

Robots assist elderly individuals with tasks like mobility support, meal preparation, and light housekeeping. These robots reduce caregiver burdens and demonstrate the real-world impact of EASE research.

Mrs. Clara, an 82-year-old woman with limited mobility, lives in an assisted living home. She recently started using a robotic assistant named "CompanionBot", designed to help with daily tasks and provide companionship.

Virtual Research Building

The VRB provides access to virtual labs for collaborative experimentation, reducing costs and promoting reproducibility. It accelerates research by enabling simulations for tasks like meal prep and household activities.

Virtual Research Building (VRB) Highlights

Digital Twin Environment: Simulates and tests robotics tasks in a controlled virtual space.

Collaborative Platform: Enables interdisciplinary and global research collaboration through VRLs.

Education and Training: Supports tutorials and immersive learning for students and researchers.

Open Science: Promotes transparency and reproducibility via openEASE integration.

Task Validation: Tests robotic tasks like object manipulation and pouring before physical deployment.

Cost-Effective Innovation: Reduces risks and costs of experimentation.

Industrial Impact: Fosters partnerships, accelerates innovation, and lowers entry barriers for businesses.
